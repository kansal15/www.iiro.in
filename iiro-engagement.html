Program Launch

A New Collaboration for Karnataka's Digital Youth

India Internet Research Organisation (IIRO) and the Internet Society Bengaluru Chapter (ISOC Bengaluru) are coming together to launch a Professional Certificate Program in Internet Governance and Digital Policy — a Youth Awareness Initiative aimed at university students and young professionals between the ages of 18 and 30 in the state of Karnataka.

The program is designed to bridge the gap between India's rapidly growing digital ecosystem and the need for an informed, engaged generation of young voices who can participate meaningfully in shaping Internet policy — at the state, national, and global level.

Delivered entirely online through live interactive sessions and structured as a cohort-based experience, this two-month program offers participants the knowledge, vocabulary, and networks that matter in today's digital governance landscape.

Comprehensive Curriculum

What the Program Covers

Six structured modules spanning the full landscape of Internet governance and digital policy — grounded in India's context and globally relevant frameworks.

Week 01

Foundations of Internet Governance

What is Internet Governance? — Definitions, scope & the multistakeholder model

Week 02

Digital Policy Landscape in India

Key Global IG Bodies — IGF, ICANN, ITU, UN mechanisms & their roles

Week 03

Cybersecurity Policy & Digital Rights

Core IG Themes — Digital access, online rights, data protection & cybersecurity

Week 04

AI, Emerging Tech & Regulation

Why Youth Voices Matter — Real examples, youth tracks, national IGF processes and Policy writing.

Week 05

Global Governance Mechanisms

Digital Rights & Online Freedoms — Privacy, surveillance, content moderation, GDPR & DPDPA.

Week 06

Advocacy, Participation & Capstone

PCybersecurity Governance — National CERTs, Budapest Convention, AI & emerging threats

Week 07

Foundations of Internet Governance

DNS, ICANN & Critical Internet Resources — How the internet's address system works

Week 08

Foundations of Internet Governance

Regional & National IG — Asia-Pacific, India's digital policy landscape, NIXI, TRAI

Program Structure

8 Weeks, Structured Learning

The program spans two months and is organised into four progressive phases — each building on the last, from foundational knowledge to practical advocacy.

Participant benefits
  • Live sessions with practitioners & policymakers
  • Access to IIRO research network & resources
  • Certificate jointly issued by IIRO & ISOC Bengaluru
  • Simulated governance forum experience

Eligibility & Audience

Designed for Karnataka's Young Digital Voices

This program is open to university students and young professionals aged 18–30 based in Karnataka — from any academic or professional background — who have an interest in technology, policy, and India's digital future.

University Students

Undergraduate and postgraduate students across disciplines — law, computer science, social sciences, engineering, public policy, business, and more.

Young Professionals

Early-career professionals from the technology sector, civil society organisations, media, public policy research, or government looking to build expertise in Internet governance.

Aspiring Policymakers

Individuals preparing for competitive examinations or careers in regulatory bodies, think tanks, digital rights organisations, or public institutions working on digital matters.

Digital Advocates

Community leaders, journalists, researchers, or NGO workers who want to understand digital policy and contribute to informed advocacy in their communities across Karnataka.

In Collaboration With

A Formal Institutional Partnership

This Professional Certificate Program is the result of a formal collaboration between two organisations committed to an open, secure, and inclusive Internet — and to nurturing the next generation of informed digital citizens in India.

IIRO
India Internet Research Organisation

IIRO is a dedicated research and policy think tank focused on studying the Internet ecosystem in India. Working on issues related to digital governance, cybersecurity, online privacy, and emerging Internet technologies, IIRO brings together researchers, volunteers, policymakers, and students to shape India's digital future through evidence-based engagement and community-driven initiatives.

iiro.in · New Delhi, India
ISOC
Internet Society Bengaluru Chapter

The Internet Society (ISOC) Bengaluru Chapter is part of the global Internet Society — a community dedicated to championing an open, globally connected, secure, and trustworthy Internet. The Bengaluru chapter actively connects members to international policy forums, runs local awareness programs, and promotes digital inclusion and capacity building across Karnataka and South India.

Internetsociety.org · Bengaluru, Karnataka
Joint Certification

Earn a Recognised Credential in Internet Policy

Upon successful completion of the program, participants receive a Professional Certificate jointly issued by IIRO and ISOC Bengaluru – a credential valued in policy research, digital rights organisations, and technology governance circles.

  • Digital badge & shareable certificate
  • Access to IIRO’s alumni community and events
  • Eligibility to join ISOC Bengaluru’s working groups
